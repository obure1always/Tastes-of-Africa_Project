Injera

A traditional Ethiopian flatbread with a unique spongy texture and slightly sour taste.

Prep Time

24 hours (fermentation)

Cook Time

30 minutes

Servings

8-10 injera

Ingredients

  • 2 cups teff flour
  • 3 cups water
  • 1/4 teaspoon active dry yeast
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 cup starter (optional, for more authentic flavor)

Instructions

  1. Mix the teff flour with water in a large bowl until smooth.
  2. Add the yeast and salt, mix well.
  3. Cover the bowl with a clean cloth and let it ferment at room temperature for 24 hours.
  4. After fermentation, the batter should be bubbly and have a slightly sour smell.
  5. Heat a large non-stick pan or traditional mitad over medium heat.
  6. Pour about 1/2 cup of batter into the center of the pan.
  7. Quickly tilt the pan in a circular motion to spread the batter evenly.
  8. Cover the pan and cook for 2-3 minutes until bubbles form and the edges start to lift.
  9. Remove the injera from the pan and place it on a clean cloth to cool.
  10. Repeat the process with the remaining batter.

Tips

  • For the best results, use authentic teff flour.
  • The longer you ferment the batter, the more sour the injera will be.
  • Keep the cooked injera covered with a cloth to prevent them from drying out.
  • Serve with traditional Ethiopian dishes like wat (stew) or tibs (saut√©ed meat).

Serving Suggestions

Injera is traditionally served with various Ethiopian dishes such as:

  • Doro Wat (spicy chicken stew)
  • Misir Wat (spiced red lentils)
  • Shiro (ground chickpea stew)
  • Gomen (collard greens)
