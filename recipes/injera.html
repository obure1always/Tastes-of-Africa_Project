Ethiopia's Beloved Sourdough Flatbread

The Heart of Ethiopian Cuisine

Injera is more than just a bread - it's a cultural icon and an essential part of Ethiopian dining. This spongy, slightly sour flatbread serves as both a plate and utensil, bringing people together around shared meals. Made from teff flour, the world's smallest grain, injera is naturally gluten-free and packed with nutrients.

Cooking Instructions
  1. Mix teff flour with water to create a smooth batter
  2. Cover and let ferment at room temperature for 2-3 days
  3. Add salt and baking soda (optional) to the fermented batter
  4. Heat a large flat pan or griddle over medium heat
  5. Pour batter in a circular motion, starting from the outside
  6. Cook until bubbles form and the edges lift (about 2-3 minutes)
  7. Remove and let cool slightly before serving

Ingredients

  • 2 cups teff flour
  • 3 cups water
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon baking soda (optional)
  • 1/4 cup starter (for first batch)
