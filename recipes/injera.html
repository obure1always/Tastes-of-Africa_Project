Injera

A traditional Ethiopian flatbread

Description

Injera is a sourdough flatbread with a slightly spongy texture, traditionally made from teff flour. It's a staple in Ethiopian cuisine and is used as both a plate and utensil.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups teff flour
  • 3 cups water
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon active dry yeast

Instructions

  1. Mix teff flour with water in a large bowl to make a thin batter.
  2. Add salt and yeast, mix well.
  3. Cover the bowl with a clean cloth and let it ferment for 2-3 days at room temperature.
  4. After fermentation, the batter should be bubbly and have a sour smell.
  5. Heat a non-stick pan or traditional mitad over medium heat.
  6. Pour a ladle of batter into the center of the pan.
  7. Quickly tilt the pan to spread the batter in a circular motion.
  8. Cover and cook for 2-3 minutes until the surface is covered with holes.
  9. Remove from heat and let cool slightly before serving.

Tips

  • The longer you ferment the batter, the more sour the injera will be
  • Make sure the pan is hot enough before pouring the batter
  • Don't flip the injera - it should cook only on one side
