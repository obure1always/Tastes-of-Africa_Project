The King of West African Cuisine

Jollof Rice is more than just a dish - it's a cultural phenomenon that brings together communities across West Africa. This vibrant, tomato-based rice dish is a staple at celebrations, family gatherings, and everyday meals. Each country has its own variation, but the essence remains the same: a perfect blend of rice, tomatoes, and aromatic spices.

Cooking Instructions
  1. Blend tomatoes, red peppers, and onions to make a smooth puree
  2. Heat oil in a large pot and sauté onions until translucent
  3. Add tomato puree and cook until reduced and darkened (about 10-15 minutes)
  4. Add tomato paste, thyme, curry powder, and bay leaves
  5. Pour in chicken stock and bring to a boil
  6. Add washed rice and stir to combine
  7. Reduce heat to low, cover, and cook for 20-25 minutes
  8. Let rest for 5 minutes before serving

Ingredients

  • 2 cups long-grain rice
  • 4 large tomatoes
  • 2 red bell peppers
  • 1 large onion
  • 3 tablespoons tomato paste
  • 2 cups chicken stock
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 1 teaspoon thyme
  • 1 teaspoon curry powder
  • 2 bay leaves
  • Salt and pepper to taste
