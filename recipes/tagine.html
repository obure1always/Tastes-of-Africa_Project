The Art of Moroccan Slow Cooking

Tagine is both the name of the dish and the distinctive earthenware pot it's cooked in. This North African specialty combines tender meat, vegetables, and aromatic spices in a slow-cooked masterpiece. The conical lid of the tagine pot helps to circulate steam, creating incredibly tender and flavorful dishes.

Cooking Instructions
  1. Season meat with spices and let marinate for 1 hour
  2. Layer vegetables in the tagine pot
  3. Place marinated meat on top of vegetables
  4. Add broth and preserved lemons
  5. Cover and cook on low heat for 2-3 hours
  6. Add olives and additional vegetables in the last 30 minutes
  7. Serve hot with couscous or bread

Ingredients

  • 2 lbs lamb or chicken, cut into pieces
  • 2 onions, sliced
  • 4 carrots, cut into chunks
  • 2 preserved lemons
  • 1 cup green olives
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 teaspoons ground cumin
  • 2 teaspoons ground coriander
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1 teaspoon ground ginger
  • 2 cups chicken or vegetable broth
  • Salt and pepper to taste
