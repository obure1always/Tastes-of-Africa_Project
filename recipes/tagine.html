Moroccan Tagine

A North African slow-cooked stew

Description

Tagine is a traditional Moroccan dish named after the earthenware pot it's cooked in. This slow-cooked stew combines meat, vegetables, and aromatic spices for a flavorful meal.

Ingredients

  • 1 kg lamb or chicken, cut into pieces
  • 2 onions, chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 carrots, sliced
  • 2 potatoes, cubed
  • 1 cup chickpeas, soaked overnight
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon ground coriander
  • 1 teaspoon ground turmeric
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh parsley for garnish

Instructions

  1. Heat olive oil in a tagine pot or heavy-bottomed pan.
  2. Brown the meat pieces on all sides and set aside.
  3. Saut√© onions and garlic until softened.
  4. Add all spices and cook for 1 minute until fragrant.
  5. Return meat to the pot and add vegetables and chickpeas.
  6. Add enough water to cover the ingredients halfway.
  7. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat to low.
  8. Cover and simmer for 2-3 hours until meat is tender.
  9. Garnish with fresh parsley before serving.

Tips

  • If you don't have a tagine pot, use a heavy-bottomed pan with a tight-fitting lid
  • Add dried fruits like apricots or prunes for a sweet touch
  • Serve with couscous or crusty bread
