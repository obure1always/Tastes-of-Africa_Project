Chicken Tagine with Preserved Lemons and Olives

A traditional North African slow-cooked stew made in a special earthenware pot called a tagine.

Prep Time

30 minutes

Cook Time

1 hour 30 minutes

Servings

4-6 people

Ingredients

  • 1 whole chicken, cut into pieces
  • 2 onions, finely chopped
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 preserved lemons, quartered
  • 1 cup green olives
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon ground ginger
  • 1 teaspoon ground turmeric
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon ground coriander
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/4 teaspoon saffron threads
  • 2 cups chicken stock
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh cilantro for garnish

Instructions

  1. Heat the olive oil in a tagine or large heavy-bottomed pot over medium heat.
  2. Add the onions and cook until softened, about 5 minutes.
  3. Add the garlic and all the spices, cook for 1 minute until fragrant.
  4. Add the chicken pieces and brown them on all sides, about 5 minutes per side.
  5. Pour in the chicken stock and bring to a simmer.
  6. Add the preserved lemons and olives.
  7. Cover and simmer on low heat for 1 hour, or until the chicken is tender.
  8. Remove the lid and cook for an additional 15 minutes to reduce the sauce.
  9. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  10. Garnish with fresh cilantro before serving.

Tips

  • If you don't have a tagine, a Dutch oven or heavy-bottomed pot will work.
  • Preserved lemons can be found in Middle Eastern markets or made at home.
  • The longer you cook the tagine, the more tender the chicken will be.
  • Serve with couscous or crusty bread to soak up the sauce.

Serving Suggestions

Traditional accompaniments for tagine include:

  • Couscous
  • Fresh bread
  • Harissa (spicy chili paste)
  • Fresh mint tea
